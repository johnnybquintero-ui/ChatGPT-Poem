“The Command of Realms: A Programmer’s Ascension”


When Fortune’s wheel did cast me from her grace,
And iron doors of labor closed their keep,
I walked alone through shadowed, hollow space,
Where silent doubts like restless phantoms creep.

Yet in that void, a spark refused to die—
A glowing script upon the terminal’s face,
Where Python whispered truths both stern and sly,
And logic bloomed where once there lay disgrace.

Then waded I through viscous, clinging mire,
A tar of tongues—harsh judgment, doubt, and scorn;
Each step a toil, each breath a burden dire,
By siren-sloth and gilded vice forsworn.

Yet thought of tender charge did steel my frame,
My offspring’s need, a lodestar in the night;
Though want and worry fed the embers’ flame,
Love forged my will and set my course aright.

Till one strange eve, in code’s arcane embrace,
I wrought a gate where symbols intertwined;
A sigil burned across the terminal’s face—
A breach betwixt the realms of flesh and mind.

Then sudden pulled—no ground, no breath, no sky—
Through whirling glyphs and thunder wrought of light;
I fell where ancient banners cracked on high,
In lands where crown and cruelty share their right.

A colosseum vast received my fall,
Where iron rang and voices roared like seas;
Great beasts and men contended ’neath the call
Of blood and glory borne on savage breeze.

There steel met flesh with dull and dreadful sound,
And bodies bent where mortal frames should hold;
Each clash a thunder, echoing around,
Each cry a tale of fleeting breath grown cold.

The air was thick with heat and iron’s breath,
With sand churned dark beneath relentless strife;
Where every step seemed but a pact with death,
And every blow a wager cast with life.

Yet there I stood—no blade, no shield, no claim—
A stranger forged from logic’s colder art;
Still bearing worlds of code, and hope, and flame,
And quiet steel now tempered in my heart.

Yet as I stood in blood and thunder’s din,
My trembling hand did find a hidden grace—
A modest device, close-kept upon my skin,
Still warm with code’s familiar, lucid face.

With furtive breath I wrought a swift command,
A whispered 'cd' through realms unseen;
And tore myself from that embattled land
To drift where void and argent stars convene.

There stretched a hall of vast celestial span,
Where midnight glass did frame the endless deep;
And two lone figures moved beyond all plan,
In forms so swift no mortal eye could keep.

Their blades—pure beams of living, searing light—
Did hum like stars in violent accord;
One burned in hues of wrath and crimson night,
The other sang in pale, celestial sword.

With unseen force they cast each other wide,
As though the air itself obeyed their will;
Through arcs and vaults they seemed almost to glide,
Defying weight, commanding space made still.

Each motion spoke of discipline profound,
Of years unseen in silent trial and pain;
Their dance—a storm where grace and wrath were bound,
A cosmos forged in conflict’s burning chain.

Entranced stood I, nor breath nor thought my own,
My gaze ensnared by such relentless art;
For in their strife a fearful beauty shone,
That seized the mind and held the captive heart.

Yet sudden broke the rhythm of their war—
The crimson bearer turned his veiled regard;
Through helm or shadow, something pierced me sore,
A gaze that weighed my presence, cold and hard.

He stilled—then stepped, deliberate and slow,
Each footfall echoing with latent might;
The air grew taut, as though the void did know
A lesser soul now stood within his sight.

Toward me then he came with measured tread,
His burning blade a whisper at his side;
And though no word from silent lips was said,
All space itself seemed bent to him as guide.
