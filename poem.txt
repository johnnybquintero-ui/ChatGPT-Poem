“The Terminal Gate: A Forked Fate in Iron Realms”


When Fortune’s wheel did cast me from her grace,
And iron doors of labor closed their keep,
I walked alone through shadowed, hollow space,
Where silent doubts like restless phantoms creep.

Yet in that void, a spark refused to die—
A glowing script upon the terminal’s face,
Where Python whispered truths both stern and sly,
And logic bloomed where once there lay disgrace.

Then waded I through viscous, clinging mire,
A tar of tongues—harsh judgment, doubt, and scorn;
Each step a toil, each breath a burden dire,
By siren-sloth and gilded vice forsworn.

Yet thought of tender charge did steel my frame,
My offspring’s need, a lodestar in the night;
Though want and worry fed the embers’ flame,
Love forged my will and set my course aright.

Till one strange eve, in code’s arcane embrace,
I wrought a gate where symbols intertwined;
A sigil burned across the terminal’s face—
A breach betwixt the realms of flesh and mind.

Then sudden pulled—no ground, no breath, no sky—
Through whirling glyphs and thunder wrought of light;
I fell where ancient banners cracked on high,
In lands where crown and cruelty share their right.

A colosseum vast received my fall,
Where iron rang and voices roared like seas;
Great beasts and men contended ’neath the call
Of blood and glory borne on savage breeze.

There steel met flesh with dull and dreadful sound,
And bodies bent where mortal frames should hold;
Each clash a thunder, echoing around,
Each cry a tale of fleeting breath grown cold.

The air was thick with heat and iron’s breath,
With sand churned dark beneath relentless strife;
Where every step seemed but a pact with death,
And every blow a wager cast with life.

Yet there I stood—no blade, no shield, no claim—
A stranger forged from logic’s colder art;
Still bearing worlds of code, and hope, and flame,
And quiet steel now tempered in my heart.
